These Echo Pods, crafted from a self-healing polymer matrix embedded with piezoelectric crystals, are affixed directly to a specially designed haptic vest. These aren't just sensors; they’re miniature resonators. Each pod is tuned to a specific range of resonant frequencies based on material composition – granite, shale, snow, wood, etc. 

Here’s the magic: as the user moves, the Echo Pods detect the vibrational energy emanating from the surrounding terrain. The piezoelectric crystals convert these vibrations into electrical signals. These signals are then fed into a miniaturized quantum processor – the 'Heartbeat' – housed within the vest. The Heartbeat analyzes the complex interplay of frequencies, identifying patterns and anomalies. This data is then translated into corresponding tactile feedback – subtle pulses, gentle vibrations, even temperature shifts – delivered directly to the user's body through the vest's micro-actuator system. Furthermore, the system utilizes a proprietary ‘Bio-Sync’ algorithm which learns the user's individual biomechanical response to different terrains, allowing for personalized feedback calibration. The entire system operates on a low-power, encrypted mesh network, transmitting data in real-time to a cloud-based analysis platform for deeper interpretation and historical data archiving.
