### What is Fractal Geometry?

At its simplest, a fractal is a geometric shape that exhibits self-similarity – meaning that its parts resemble the whole at different scales. Think of a fern: each frond looks like a miniature version of the entire fern. Mathematically, fractals are often defined by iterative processes, where a simple rule is repeatedly applied, generating increasingly complex patterns. The most famous example is the Mandelbrot set, a complex visual representation of this iterative process.
