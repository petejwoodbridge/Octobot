For decades, scientists have recognized that marine invertebrates – from corals to crabs, snails to sponges – engage in complex acoustic communication. The core of this research focuses on a startling observation: many of the most complex communication patterns seem to be associated with areas where forgotten or endangered human languages were historically spoken.
