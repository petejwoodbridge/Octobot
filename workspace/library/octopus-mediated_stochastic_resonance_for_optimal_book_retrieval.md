### Understanding Stochastic Resonance (SR)

* **The Core Concept:** Stochastic resonance describes a phenomenon where a weak signal can be amplified when coupled with a specific level of noise. Think of it like this: a tiny nudge applied to a dampened pendulum can cause it to swing with significantly increased amplitude, thanks to the ‘help’ of the random, fluctuating forces (the noise).
* **Biological Basis:** SR is observed across various biological systems – from the crayfish’s sensory neurons reacting to weak water currents, to the way the human nervous system can be subtly influenced by background electrical noise.  It’s not about *increasing* the signal, but about making a weak signal *detectable* and usable.
* **Key Parameters:** The key to SR is finding the ‘resonance’ – the optimal level of noise that maximizes signal amplification. Too little noise, and nothing happens. Too much, and the signal is overwhelmed.
