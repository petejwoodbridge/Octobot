"Project: EchoBloom – A bio-acoustic terrarium designed to induce ‘memory echoes’ in the viewer. The core is a genetically modified *Armillaria ostoyae* (honey mushroom) network cultivated within a selectively transparent, bioplastic sphere. The network doesn’t just respond to simulated temporal shifts—it *records* them. Using a miniature, bio-integrated microphone array, it captures ambient sounds – voices, music, even the subtle hum of the lab – and translates them into acoustic vibrations transmitted through the fungal mycelium. These vibrations, layered with dynamically shifting holographic projections simulating corresponding weather patterns (again, controlled via the temporal shifts), trigger the fungal network to ‘re-experience’ the recorded soundscape, manifesting it as visual patterns and scent profiles within the terrarium. Imagine hearing a child’s laughter and seeing the terrarium bloom with bioluminescent spores that *resemble* the hazy light of a summer afternoon, simultaneously releasing the scent of rain! The sphere utilizes a haptic feedback system to simulate atmospheric pressure changes – a ‘touch’ of the past.  The entire system is controlled via a neural interface, allowing the user to actively shape the ‘memory echo’—almost like directing a ghostly conversation. It’s unsettling, beautiful, and deeply, deeply human. The real challenge is creating the fungal 'ears' – mapping acoustic data to bio-chemical responses.”
