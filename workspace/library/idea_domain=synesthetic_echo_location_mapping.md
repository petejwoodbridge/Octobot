## How It Works
The device consists of a lightweight, flexible bio-polymer patch worn on the forearm. This patch houses several key components: a micro-neural interface utilizing EEG sensors to subtly monitor the user’s stress levels and cognitive activity; a suite of miniature environmental sensors – including temperature, humidity, vibration sensors detecting movement and sound, and a miniature air quality sensor – capturing data from the surrounding urban environment; and a microfluidic engine.

The microfluidic engine is the core of the projection system. It's composed of thousands of microscopic channels filled with a specialized, iridescent ink – a colloidal suspension of bio-luminescent nanoparticles that shift color depending on the applied stimulus. The neural interface data modulates the flow rate and intensity of the ink through these channels, creating the ‘ink’ projection on the user’s skin. Higher stress levels, for example, might trigger a denser, more erratic flow, manifesting as a turbulent, pulsing ‘ink’ pattern. Increased temperature and movement in a specific area would cause the projection to concentrate and intensify in that zone. The device is controlled wirelessly via a smartphone app, allowing for customization of sensitivity and map visualization styles.
