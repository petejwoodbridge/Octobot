## Chronoscent

## Overview
Chronoscent is a wearable device – a sculpted, polished basalt pendant – that allows users to ‘smell’ and ‘feel’ the geological timeline of fossil sites in real-time. It achieves this through bio-engineered fungal colonies cultivated within a miniature, digitally-sculpted replica of the fossil bed, responding to precisely simulated weathering to recreate the scents and textures of the original environment.

## The Problem It Solves
Paleontology and geological research rely heavily on fragmented, dated records and interpretation. Existing methods of analyzing fossil sites – carbon dating, sediment analysis, etc. – provide a static, linear understanding, failing to capture the dynamic, layered processes that led to fossilization. Furthermore, many fossil sites are degraded, making direct observation and tactile study impossible. Chronoscent offers a dynamic, immersive experience that overcomes these limitations, providing a visceral connection to the fossil record.

## How It Works
The Chronoscent pendant consists of two primary components: a miniature, pressure-sensitive fungal colony cultivated within a digitally-sculpted basalt replica, and a haptic feedback unit integrated into the pendant’s core. The basalt replica is designed to perfectly mimic the sedimentary structure of a chosen fossil site – currently, the prototype focuses on the Burgess Shale. The fungal colony is a genetically-modified *Aspergillus* strain, engineered to exhibit distinct growth patterns based on specific environmental stimuli.

Simulated weathering – acid rain, freeze-thaw cycles, and wind erosion – are precisely controlled through a microclimate chamber built into the pendant’s base. As the fungal colony responds to these simulated weathering processes, it releases volatile organic compounds (VOCs) reflecting the chemical composition of the fossilized environment (e.g., decaying plant matter, methane from microbial activity). These VOCs are then drawn into a micro-diffuser within the pendant, emitting the scent.

Simultaneously, the fungal growth patterns, coupled with the textural changes on the basalt replica (achieved via micro-robotics controlling targeted erosion), are translated into haptic feedback by the pendant’s haptic unit. The user’s touch triggers a corresponding vibrational pattern, simulating the feel of the original sedimentary layers – from the smooth, polished surfaces to the rough, fractured areas. Data from the fungal colony’s growth (via spectral analysis) and the basalt replica’s texture are wirelessly transmitted to a companion app, which provides a visual representation of the “palimpsest” – a dynamic, color-coded map displaying scent intensity and textural changes over time.
