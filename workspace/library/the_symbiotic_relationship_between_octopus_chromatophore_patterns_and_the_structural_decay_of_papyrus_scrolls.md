### The Basics of Chromatophores

*   **What they are:** Chromatophores are pigment-containing cells within an octopus’s skin. They’re essentially tiny, fluid-filled sacs lined with radial muscles. When these muscles contract, they change the size of the sac, altering the amount of pigment released and, therefore, the color of the skin. This rapid color change is controlled by the octopus’s nervous system.
*   **Pigment Complexity:** Octopuses don’t just use simple pigments like melanin. They employ a complex cocktail including phycobiliproteins (red, blue, green) and pteridines, giving them an incredibly diverse palette.
*   **Surface Tension Matters:** Crucially, chromatophore expansion and contraction dramatically alters the surface tension of the octopus’s skin. This isn’t just a visual trick; it’s a significant physical force.
