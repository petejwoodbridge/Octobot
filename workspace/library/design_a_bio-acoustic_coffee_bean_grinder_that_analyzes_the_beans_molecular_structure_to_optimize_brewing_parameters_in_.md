## The Problem It Solves
Traditional coffee grinding is a notoriously imprecise art. Even experienced baristas often struggle to consistently extract the ideal flavor from coffee beans due to variations in bean density, moisture, and even subtle changes in the bean's cellular structure—factors undetectable by human senses. Existing acoustic coffee brewing systems focus on the *output* of the brewed coffee, not the critical input of the grinding process, leaving significant room for improvement in optimizing flavor from the very beginning.

## How It Works
The Sonaris Brew’s core is a micro-grinder chamber lined with a series of piezoelectric transducers. If they're too dense, the grinder slows down and the burr size decreases for finer grinding.  A color-changing LED on the grinder’s casing visually indicates the optimal extraction parameters – green for ideal, yellow for slight adjustments, red for needing a manual override. The system connects wirelessly to a companion app for logging preferences and bean data. Crucially, it builds upon existing bio-acoustic brewing research, taking a granular, active approach to sound manipulation in bean preparation.
