## How It Works

The GeoSonar™ utilizes a three-stage process, all housed within a sleek, walnut-encased device approximately the size of a large coffee table. 

**Stage 1: Bioacoustic Resonance Mapping:** The geode is placed within the GeoSonar’s core – a spherical chamber lined with piezoelectric sensors. The device then emits a precisely calibrated sequence of ultra-low frequency acoustic waves (below 20Hz) – frequencies known to penetrate crystalline structures deeply. These waves aren’t just sound; they’re a carefully orchestrated ‘probe’ designed to stimulate resonant vibrations within the geode’s minerals.  The piezoelectric sensors meticulously record the subtle changes in pressure and vibration caused by these resonant frequencies.

**Stage 2: Crystalline Mapping & Algorithmic Translation:** This data is fed into our proprietary “ChronosCore” – a miniature quantum computer (yes, really!) that performs a complex crystalline mapping analysis. Based on a vast database of mineral acoustic signatures (built over 15 years of research into the resonance characteristics of various geological formations worldwide), the ChronosCore identifies the dominant minerals within the geode, their relative proportions, and, crucially, their *age*.  We’ve developed an algorithm that translates the specific resonant frequencies back into a series of musical notes and harmonic textures – essentially, the geode ‘speaks’ through music.

**Stage 3: Dynamic Sonification & Temporal Layering:** Finally, the ChronosCore synthesizes the identified data into a continuously evolving soundscape.  This isn't a static recording; the sound changes subtly over time, mirroring geological processes – slow shifts in mineral composition reflecting tectonic plate movement, the ‘pulse’ of ancient hydrothermal activity, even the subtle erosion of time itself. The user controls the intensity and layering of the soundscape via a touch-sensitive interface, allowing them to focus on specific temporal facets of the geode’s history.
