### The Foundation: Acoustic Learning in Cephalopods

For decades, scientists understood cephalopods (squid, cuttlefish, and octopuses) primarily relied on visual cues for orientation and memory. However, mounting evidence has revealed a significant role for acoustics. It’s now believed that many cephalopod species, particularly octopuses, utilize underwater sounds – particularly those created by their own movements and the surrounding environment – to construct detailed cognitive maps.

**Key Fact:** Octopuses have specialized structures in their heads – the *mantle ridges* – that contain highly sensitive receptors capable of detecting subtle changes in pressure and sound. These aren’t just for hearing; they’re finely tuned to the vibrations created by *their own movements* and interactions with the environment.
