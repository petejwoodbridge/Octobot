## How It Works
The Navigator consists of a micro-reservoir device, roughly the size of a large button, that’s seamlessly integrated into a flexible, breathable skin patch. This patch is cultivated with *Luminomyces*, a genetically modified lichen strain. *Luminomyces* are engineered to exhibit bioluminescence based on specific biochemical responses to the user's physiological data, primarily heart rate variability, skin conductance, and cortisol levels – all gathered through integrated biosensors within the device. Simultaneously, a competing fungal strain, *Mycelium Maximus*, is introduced into the reservoir. *Mycelium Maximus* is engineered to rapidly grow and consume nutrients when stimulated by *Luminomyces*’ bioluminescence. The user’s emotional state directly affects the intensity of the lichen's glow *and* the availability of nutrients for the fungus, accelerating or inhibiting its growth. A sophisticated algorithm analyzes the bioluminescent patterns and fungal growth rate, translating this data into points. Users compete globally via a dedicated app, building a leaderboard of ‘Myco-Masters’ based on speed and pattern complexity.
