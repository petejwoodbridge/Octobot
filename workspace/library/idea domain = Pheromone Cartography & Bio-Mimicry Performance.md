Echo Bloom is a bio-reactive installation that transforms ambient sound into personalized olfactory experiences. Utilizing a network of miniature, genetically-modified ‘Scent-Cells’ housed within a sculpted, illuminated shell, the device analyzes incoming audio and generates corresponding aromas – recreating moments, evoking emotions, or even simply adding a beautiful, unexpected layer to the environment. It’s like experiencing sound with your nose.

## How It Works

This visual feedback reinforces the immersive experience. 

The Scent-Cell Array houses 100 genetically-modified *Rosa odorata* (rose) cells, each engineered with a unique gene sequence triggered by specific sound frequencies. These cells aren’t simply releasing perfume; they’re synthesizing complex volatile organic compounds (VOCs) – aromas – based on the analyzed sound. The Bio-Acoustic Processor, a sophisticated neural network chip, analyzes incoming audio in real-time, translating sonic patterns (bass, treble, rhythm) into precise bio-chemical signals that activate the corresponding Scent-Cells. The level of intensity and specific scent profiles are dynamically adjusted based on the complexity and emotional tone of the sound. Calibration uses a library of pre-programmed scent ‘signatures’ for common sounds (laughter, rain, music) alongside a machine-learning algorithm to personalize responses over time.
