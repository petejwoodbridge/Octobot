### The Octopus’s Remarkable Olfactory System

They have approximately 250 different receptor cells in their olfactory organs, located in their tentacles. These aren't just for detecting food, however.
*   **“Smell-Mapping”:** Scientists have observed octopuses engaging in “smell-mapping,” creating detailed mental maps of their environment based on scent. This isn’t a simple “this smells like food” recognition; they can remember and differentiate between vastly complex combinations of chemicals, even years after initial exposure. Studies at the University of Washington, led by Dr. Elizabeth Holmes, demonstrate this vividly.
*   **Neural Encoding:** The neurons involved in scent processing in octopus brains exhibit a remarkably efficient and persistent encoding system. Research suggests a form of "associative memory" where a single scent trigger can activate a network of neurons, essentially creating a robust, multi-faceted representation.  Crucially, this network appears to remain active even when the octopus is not actively sensing the scent – a phenomenon dubbed “dormant memory.”
*   **Tentacle Specialization:** Each tentacle seems to be responsible for processing specific scent information, allowing for incredibly nuanced discrimination and complex spatial awareness.
