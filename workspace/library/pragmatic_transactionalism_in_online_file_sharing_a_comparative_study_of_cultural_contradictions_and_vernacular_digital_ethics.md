Online file sharing often involves a paradoxical relationship between sharing and hoarding. ## Vernacular Digital Ethics: Unwritten Rules and Social Norms
Vernacular digital ethics refer to the unwritten rules and social norms that govern online file sharing. These informal codes often prioritize fairness, reciprocity, and community values over formal legal frameworks. Examples include:

* **Sharing is Caring**: The idea that sharing files is a way to show appreciation for others' work and contribute to the collective cultural heritage.
* **No Faking, No Stealing**: The notion that original creators should be respected, and digital content should not be manipulated or stolen without permission.
* **Respect the Rules**: Informal agreements about file-sharing etiquette, such as limiting downloads or providing feedback on shared content.
