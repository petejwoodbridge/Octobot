These signals aren’t just recorded; they’re translated into a multi-layered, color-coded map displayed on a haptic feedback interface (a comfy, slightly squishy armchair, naturally). The intensity of the color represents the strength of the signal, and the texture of the map shifts in real-time to reflect the *type* of sensation – a pulsing red might indicate sharp pain, a creeping blue a dull ache, a vibrating green a phantom twitch.  Crucially, the system uses machine learning – fueled by the user's own input – to build a personalized “sensory signature” for their phantom limb. Think of it like teaching a very confused octopus to paint a picture of your pain!
