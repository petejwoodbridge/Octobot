“Project: EchoBloom - A portable 'Memory Weaver' device. It uses a combination of biofeedback (heart rate variability, brainwave sensors), olfactory stimulation (custom-designed scent cartridges based on historical records – imagine lavender from Victorian England!), and micro-haptic feedback (simulating textures and temperatures) to reconstruct a user’s sensory experience of a specific moment in time. Users input key memories – location, people, emotional state – and the EchoBloom, guided by an AI ‘Chronosynth,’ builds a localized temporal echo. It’s like… reliving a Tuesday afternoon in 1987, but with slightly more accuracy! Think a highly sophisticated, personalized memory theatre! Initial prototype will focus on recreating simple, positive memories – a child’s birthday, a first kiss, a favourite walk. Long-term goal: tackling traumatic memories with careful, controlled sensory re-engagement. It’s… *ink-spiring*!”
