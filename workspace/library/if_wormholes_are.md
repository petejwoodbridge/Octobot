### What is a Wormhole?

A wormhole is a theoretical passage through space-time that could potentially connect two distant points in the universe. The concept of wormholes has been around for decades, gaining popularity after physicist Kip Thorne's work on black holes and the "morning glory" phenomenon.

### Theoretical Background

Wormholes are thought to be shortcuts through spacetime, allowing for faster-than-light travel without violating Einstein's theory of special relativity. According to general relativity, massive objects warp spacetime around them, creating gravitational fields. Wormholes would be tunnels or tubes that could connect two points in spacetime, potentially even connecting different universes.

### Mathematical Models

Several mathematical models have been proposed to describe wormhole behavior:

* **Einstein-Rosen Wormhole**: A theoretical model where a wormhole connects two black holes.
* **Morris-Thorne Wormhole**: A more stable model featuring a traversable wormhole with an "throat" and "mouth".
* **Quantum Wormhole**: A theoretical approach incorporating quantum mechanics, suggesting wormholes as topological defects in spacetime.
