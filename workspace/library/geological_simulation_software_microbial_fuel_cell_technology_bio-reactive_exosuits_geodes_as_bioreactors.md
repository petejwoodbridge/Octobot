## How It Works
The core technology involves three key components: Genetically Engineered Geodes (GEGs), Bio-Reactive Polymers (BRPs), and a Micro-Control Matrix (MCM). GEGs are large, porous geodes cultivated with a consortium of extremophile bacteria, primarily *Shewanella* and *Geobacter*, engineered to efficiently convert geothermal heat into electrical energy. These bacteria are housed in a specially formulated nutrient broth enriched with dissolved minerals extracted from the geode itself.

BRPs are a novel, rapidly self-assembling polymer derived from bacterial cellulose and modified fungal mycelium. When introduced to the GEG's electrical current, the BRPs flow into the geode’s hollow spaces, forming a structurally robust shell around the bacterial colony. The thickness and density of the BRP shell are controlled by adjusting the electrical current – thicker shells provide more protection, thinner shells allow for greater flexibility.

The MCM, a network of microscopic sensors and microfluidic channels embedded within the BRP shell, monitors the wearer’s vital signs (temperature, stress levels, muscle activity) and adjusts the electrical current to the GEG, influencing bacterial activity and, consequently, the BRP shell’s properties. The bacteria also produce bioluminescent compounds, creating a soft, pulsating light show controlled by the MCM.  The entire system is designed to be largely self-repairing - damaged BRP can be stimulated by the electrical current to rapidly regenerate.
