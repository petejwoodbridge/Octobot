The MycoScent utilizes bio-acoustic mapping and subsurface fungal network analysis to identify chemical signatures associated with specific industrial processes. These signatures – heavy metals, solvents, plastics – are then synthesized into bespoke perfume formulations by a micro-reactor system housed within a portable, geodesic dome. Users don a haptic suit that translates the fungal network’s vibrational patterns into tactile sensations – tremors, pulses, even simulated ground movement. The olfactory output, controlled via a neural interface, evokes memories and emotions linked to the industrial site's past, layering scents of rust, machine oil, and decaying plastic with evocative, psychologically-designed fragrance notes. Calibration is achieved through a proprietary ‘memory mapping’ process, where users detail their emotional responses to the initial scent profiles – creating a personalized olfactory fingerprint. The dome itself is constructed from bio-luminescent mycelium, pulsating with light synchronized to the fungal network's activity. Think ‘Inception’ meets ‘Silent Hill’ meets… well, a really, *really* smelly factory!”
