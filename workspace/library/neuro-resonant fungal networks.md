## How It Works

The NRFN utilizes a three-stage process. First, a personalized "Mycelial Seed” – a culture of *Mycena luxaeterna* spores genetically modified to express specific bioluminescent proteins and vibrational frequencies – is cultivated within a sterile, temperature-controlled chamber. This seed is then introduced into the ceramic casing, where it rapidly forms a complex, three-dimensional network. Crucially, we’ve incorporated micro-scale piezoelectric transducers within the fungal hyphae. These transducers convert subtle shifts in the mycelial network’s vibrational state – induced by external stimuli like music, light, or even intention – into electrical signals. 

Second, the network interfaces with the user through a non-invasive “Resonance Patch” – a flexible, graphene-based patch embedded with bio-compatible sensors. This patch is applied to the user’s scalp, strategically positioned to maximize neural stimulation. The sensors detect the electrical signals from the fungal network and, using a miniature, bio-integrated processor, translate these signals into tailored sensory experiences – bursts of color, gentle vibrations, and subtly shifting auditory patterns. 

Finally, the processor feeds back into the fungal network, modulating its vibrational output in real-time, creating a dynamic, resonant loop.  We've even integrated a system of microfluidic channels within the ceramic casing to deliver targeted doses of neuro-stimulating compounds derived from the fungi, amplifying the effect. The entire system is controlled via a neural interface app, allowing users to customize their experience and monitor their neural activity.
