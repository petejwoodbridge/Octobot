The human brain’s connection between smell and memory is incredibly strong – often more vivid and emotional than visual or auditory recollections. However, accessing these memories can be difficult, relying on fragmented triggers like photographs or songs. Chronospores offer a direct, biometrically-driven route to specific moments, alleviating the frustration of chasing fading recollections and potentially aiding in therapeutic recall.
