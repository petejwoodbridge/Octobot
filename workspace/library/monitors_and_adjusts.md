## Overview
ChromaSync is a biofeedback-integrated monitor that subtly adjusts your screen’s color temperature and brightness based on your subconscious emotional state, promoting optimal focus and reducing eye strain. It's a wearable device—a sleek, wrist-mounted band—that analyzes your heart rate variability and subtle skin conductivity changes to provide dynamic visual comfort.

## The Problem It Solves
Prolonged screen time, particularly with devices emitting blue light, is linked to digital eye strain, headaches, reduced sleep quality, and even increased anxiety. Current solutions like blue light filters are often static and don't adapt to the *actual* intensity of your emotional response to what you’re seeing – a stressful presentation versus a relaxing nature documentary will both trigger a blue light response, but require vastly different corrective measures.

## How It Works
ChromaSync uses a multi-pronged approach. Firstly, the wristband contains a miniature PPG (photoplethysmography) sensor to constantly monitor heart rate variability, a key indicator of stress levels. Secondly, a micro-E-Skin sensor within the band detects subtle changes in skin conductivity – a measure of neurotransmitter activity associated with emotional states. This data is fed into a miniature, low-power AI chip, specifically trained on a vast database of biometric responses correlated with emotional states. 

The AI then communicates wirelessly with a connected monitor via a proprietary protocol, sending precise instructions to the monitor's color temperature and brightness settings. The monitor isn’t just dimming; it’s subtly shifting the hue – warming the screen for feelings of anxiety, subtly cooling it for boredom, and enhancing blue hues for moments of focus.  A haptic feedback system in the wristband provides gentle vibrations to indicate changes and guide the user to a state of optimal visual comfort. It also tracks usage and provides personalized recommendations based on your unique biometric profile.
