### What are Wormholes?

Wormholes are hypothetical tunnels through spacetime that could connect two distant points in space. They're like cosmic shortcuts that potentially allow for faster-than-light travel and communication. The concept was first proposed by physicist Albert Einstein and his colleague Nathan Rosen in 1935.

### How do Wormholes Work?

Imagine a tunnel or tube connecting two points in spacetime, with one end entering our universe and the other emerging in another universe or dimension. This "throat" of the wormhole would be stabilized by a type of exotic matter with negative energy density.

As particles or even spacecraft approach the entrance to the wormhole, they'd experience an intense gravitational pull, causing them to accelerate rapidly towards the tunnel's center. Once inside, the tunnel's curvature would bend spacetime around the objects, allowing them to travel vast distances in a relatively short period.
