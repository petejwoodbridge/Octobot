## Overview

It doesn’t just identify the compounds; it recreates the *feeling* of a landslide, the heat of a volcanic eruption, or the slow, silent decay of a fossil – offering an unprecedented, visceral connection to Earth’s history.

## The Problem It Solves
Paleontologists, geologists, and archaeologists currently rely on indirect methods – interpreting sediment layers, analyzing fossilized remains, and conducting simulations – to understand geological events. These methods are often imprecise and fail to provide a truly immersive understanding of the conditions surrounding a significant geological moment. Furthermore, experiencing these events through purely visual or textual representations can leave a critical sensory dimension missing – the smell of ancient water interacting with stone, the sulfurous fumes of volcanic ash, or the decaying organic matter of a fossilized forest.

## How It Works

Once the VOC profile is determined, the microfluidic system rapidly synthesizes a precise mixture of these VOCs. The intensity and duration of the scent are controlled via a haptic interface on the device’s handle, allowing users to adjust the ‘temporal depth’ of the reconstruction – from a fleeting impression to a sustained, immersive experience.
