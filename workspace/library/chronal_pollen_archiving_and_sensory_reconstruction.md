## How It Works
The Chronoscent consists of three core components. Firstly, a miniaturized, highly sensitive micro-spectrometer constantly analyzes the ambient air, searching for trace amounts of pollen. Crucially, we’ve developed a global database of pollen signatures correlated to specific historical events – painstakingly compiled through archaeological analysis, atmospheric modeling, and historical records. This database identifies pollen types most prevalent during, say, the construction of the Roman Colosseum or the bustle of a 19th-century London coffee house.

Secondly, once a historical pollen signature is identified, the device utilizes a sophisticated olfactory delivery system. Thousands of tiny, perfectly calibrated capsules filled with synthesized scent molecules are precisely released into the air, recreating the original aroma. These molecules are created via advanced molecular reconstruction techniques – essentially, synthesizing the chemical compounds present in the historical scents.

Finally, the Chronoscent integrates a non-invasive neural interface – a lightweight, bio-compatible headband – which subtly stimulates the olfactory bulb. This amplification is controlled via a sophisticated algorithm that adjusts based on the user's individual sensory profile, maximizing the perceived intensity and realism of the scent experience. Furthermore, binaural audio recreating the appropriate ambient sounds – from Roman legions marching to Victorian carriages – is layered alongside the scent to create a fully immersive environment.
