*   **Discovery:** In 2006, researchers led by Dr. Justin Froehlich observed *Riftia pachyptila* – giant tube worms thriving around hydrothermal vents in the Atlantic – repeatedly extending and retracting their feeding plumes. *   **The Repeat:** The key observation wasn’t just the extension/retraction, but the *frequency* and *predictability* of this behavior. The worms would perform the action hundreds, sometimes thousands, of times within a 24-hour period.
*   **Initial Hypothesis:** Froehlich proposed that this behavior was akin to a “compulsion” – a seemingly pointless, repetitive action driven by an underlying anxiety or discomfort. This sparked the now-popular term "tube worm OCD."
