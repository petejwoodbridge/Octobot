### I. Buddhist Foundations – The Web of Dependent Origination

* **Dependent Origination (Pratītyasamutpāda):** This is *the* foundational principle. It posits that everything arises in dependence upon conditions. Nothing exists in isolation. Every action, every thought, every being, is inextricably linked to countless other factors, creating a vast, constantly shifting web of cause and effect.  It’s not simply “connectedness” in a philosophical sense; it’s a dynamic, energetic process.
* **Emptiness (Śūnyatā):** Closely related, emptiness doesn't mean “nothing.” It means that phenomena lack inherent, independent existence. They are *conditioned*—meaning their existence is defined by their relationships with other things.  This profoundly impacts the concept of ‘self,’ suggesting a fluid, relational identity.
* **Universal Compassion (Karuna):** The recognition that all beings experience suffering, and therefore, are worthy of care and understanding, naturally flows from the interconnectedness framework. If all beings are part of the same web, harming one ultimately harms all.
