### Slithering Through Complexity

* Earthworms (Lumbricus terrestris) are a popular species for studying worm behavior.

* Worm bodies consist of repeated segments (setae), allowing them to flexibly navigate through soil and debris.

## Sensory Systems
### Sensitive to the Environment

* Worms possess a range of sensory organs:
	+ Chemosensors detect chemical cues, such as food and predators.
	+ Mechoreceptors respond to touch, vibrations, and mechanical stimuli.
	+ Hydroreceptors perceive water quality and movement.
* These sensory systems enable worms to navigate, find food, and avoid threats.

## Social Behaviour
### Worms in Harmony

* Worms often live in close proximity, forming aggregations (worm "cities").
* They communicate through chemical signals:
	+ Pheromones convey information about food sources, predators, and social status.
	+ Hormonal signals regulate reproductive cycles and territorial behavior.
* Cooperative behaviors include shared burrow maintenance and predator avoidance.
