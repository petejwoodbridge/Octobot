First, a ‘Geological Echo’ is captured. This isn’t simply a recording; it’s a complex sonorous map. We utilize a network of ultra-sensitive geophones strategically placed within the target geological location – let’s say, the Icelandic Highlands – to record the subtle vibrational frequencies emanating from the rock, the wind, the water, and even the geothermal activity. This data is then fed into our proprietary ‘Tempus Bloom’ – a genetically-engineered species of *Mycena* fungi. 

These fungi aren't just pretty; they’re bio-acoustic processors. They’ve been engineered to interpret the geological data and synthesize it into a complex, layered sonicscape. The soundscape isn’t just audio; it’s ‘resonant sculpting’. The dome’s polymer structure subtly vibrates in response to the fungi’s output, creating a haptic feedback layer. Simultaneously, a carefully calibrated olfactory layering system – using volatile organic compounds derived from plants indigenous to the captured location – provides a subtle aromatic experience. Finally, the entire system is controlled by the ‘Myth Archetype Wearable,’ a biofeedback device that monitors the user's neurological state. Based on this data, it dynamically adjusts the sonic, tactile, and olfactory parameters, ensuring the experience remains optimally calming and deeply immersive. Think of it as a personalized, geological dream machine.
