*   **Circadian Rhythm Baseline:** The human circadian rhythm, roughly a 24-hour cycle, dictates many physiological processes, including hormone levels, sleep-wake cycles, and cognitive function. *   **Cortisol Levels:** Cortisol, a stress hormone, is typically highest in the morning and declines throughout the day. Elevated cortisol can impair cognitive functions like complex problem-solving and attention – potentially impacting the deep concentration needed for sustained silent reading.
*   **Melatonin Production:** Melatonin, produced primarily at night, regulates sleep. Interestingly, preliminary data suggests a *positive* correlation between evening melatonin levels (within a healthy range) and the ability to process abstract information – perhaps linked to a state of relaxed receptivity.
