Existing therapy techniques can be challenging to access and may not always effectively translate complex emotional data into tangible, understandable formats.

Mnemosyne identifies recurring patterns and subtle shifts in biometric signals associated with memory fragments. These fragments aren't just identified; they are *reconstructed* – essentially, the AI builds a three-dimensional model of the emotional context surrounding the memory.

This reconstruction then drives a bespoke generative art engine. The engine utilizes a chaotic fractal algorithm, typically reliant on complex mathematical equations, but *modulated* by the reconstructed emotional data. For example, a fragmented memory of joy might generate a vibrant, expanding fractal filled with shimmering, high-frequency tones, while a memory of sadness could produce a slower, more contained fractal with lower frequencies and muted, melancholic sounds. The bioluminescence is achieved through miniature, biocompatible LEDs embedded within the fractal patterns, creating a captivating, dynamic visual display.
