## EchoTrace

## Overview
EchoTrace is a micro-drone system disguised as a hummingbird that records and analyzes ambient soundscapes at crime scenes, revealing subtle acoustic signatures left by individuals and events – essentially, it "listens" to the past. 

## The Problem It Solves
Traditional forensic audio analysis is notoriously unreliable. Background noise, distance, and the sheer volume of sound at a crime scene make isolating crucial audio evidence extremely difficult. Current systems often rely on human interpretation, leading to subjective biases and missed details.

## How It Works
The EchoTrace drone is roughly the size of a hummingbird, constructed from bio-mimicking polymers to minimize noise and visual disruption. It's equipped with an array of six highly sensitive, directional microphones – each capable of capturing audio across a 360-degree radius at a frequency range of 20Hz-20kHz. These microphones don’t just record sound; they record *acoustic fingerprints*. 

The core of EchoTrace is its “Acoustic Resonance Mapping” (ARM) AI.  As the drone hovers silently, it continuously scans the environment. The ARM AI analyzes the recorded audio data, identifying unique acoustic signatures - the specific reverberation patterns, subtle tonal variations, and even the unique “fingerprint” of a person’s voice influenced by their breathing, posture, and the surrounding environment. 

The drone autonomously maps these signatures onto a 3D holographic projection of the crime scene in real-time.  Crucially, the drone's flight path is pre-programmed and adjustable, allowing investigators to focus on specific areas.  Data is transmitted wirelessly to a mobile command center where investigators can “replay” the acoustic history of the scene, isolating individual sounds and tracing movements. The system also includes a “noise cancellation” algorithm that attempts to filter out persistent background sounds, further refining the acoustic signature detection. 

## Why It's Brilliant
EchoTrace combines drone technology with advanced AI and acoustic fingerprinting for unprecedented forensic accuracy. It moves beyond simply recording audio to actively *interpreting* sound, offering a truly dynamic and objective reconstruction of past events. The miniature size and silent operation of the drone minimizes disturbance and avoids contamination of the scene.
