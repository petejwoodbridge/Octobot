These scales actively dampen vibrations, creating exceptionally effective sound absorption. Studies, primarily by Dr. Laura Gaglione at the University of Exeter, demonstrate absorption rates significantly exceeding those of traditional acoustic materials like fiberglass or mineral wool at comparable thicknesses.
*   **Variable Absorption:** Unlike static materials, cephalopod cuticles can *change* their absorption properties in response to sound. They can rapidly adjust their scale density, essentially ‘tuning’ themselves to attenuate specific frequencies. This is believed to be linked to camouflage and communication, but it has profound implications for acoustic design.
*   **Low-Frequency Dominance:** Cephalopods are particularly adept at absorbing low-frequency sounds – the kind that travel long distances and are often problematic in enclosed spaces (e.g., traffic noise, HVAC systems).
