The Stardust Weaver is a compact, handheld device that analyzes ambient light and atmospheric pressure to synthesize microscopic, bioluminescent particles mimicking the spectral composition of stardust. These particles are then projected onto surfaces, creating a personalized, dynamic aurora borealis effect that shifts and changes based on the surrounding environment and user preferences.

## How It Works
The Stardust Weaver utilizes a miniaturized spectrometer housed within a polished obsidian casing. This spectrometer analyzes incoming light, specifically identifying the wavelengths present in the ambient environment (sunlight, moonlight, artificial light). Simultaneously, it measures atmospheric pressure, humidity, and even micro-vibrations – all influencing the dispersal of light.

These algae have been engineered to produce a range of bioluminescent pigments matching the wavelengths detected. A focused holographic projector then captures these particles and directs them outwards, creating the stardust effect.  Users control the intensity, color palette, and movement patterns through a haptic interface on the device itself, allowing for real-time interaction.  Advanced algorithms ensure the synthesized stardust particles blend seamlessly with the existing light, creating a believable and dynamic spectacle.
