Crucially, these vibrations are then processed by a proprietary Neuro-Harmonic Translator (NHT) – a quantum-entangled processor – which analyzes the vibrational data and converts it into olfactory and tactile stimuli. Different mineral compositions trigger distinct scents – quartz might evoke the sharp, cool air of a glacial valley, while basalt produces the earthy, mineral aroma of volcanic landscapes. Tactile feedback is generated through precisely controlled micro-vibrations, mimicking the feeling of stone shifting beneath your hand, the pressure of ancient ice, or the subtle tremors of tectonic movement. The entire system is wirelessly linked to a central database, allowing for real-time geological data analysis and “memory-scape” calibration.
