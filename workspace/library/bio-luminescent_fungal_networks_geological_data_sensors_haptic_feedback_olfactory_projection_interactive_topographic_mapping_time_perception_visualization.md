Traditional geological surveys rely on static data, offering limited emotional connection to the deep past. Existing time perception visualization tools are largely digital and detached, failing to tap into the body's inherent understanding of sequence and rhythm. Furthermore, interpreting subtle shifts in geological formations requires constant, intense focus – a draining and often inaccurate process.

It’s a tool for geological research, exploration, and even art, fundamentally shifting our relationship with the landscape.
