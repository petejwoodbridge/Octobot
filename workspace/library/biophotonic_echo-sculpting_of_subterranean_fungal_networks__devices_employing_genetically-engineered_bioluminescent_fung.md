We’ve been missing a crucial piece of the puzzle, a way to genuinely *understand* this foundational, silent communication system.

## How It Works
Mycelial Resonance centers around three exquisitely intertwined technologies. First, we cultivate a bespoke strain of *Mycena luxnova*, a genetically-engineered bioluminescent fungus, within intricately designed 3D-printed mycelial matrices. These matrices aren’t just containers; they’re specifically architected to maximize bio-acoustic sensitivity – each chamber is shaped to amplify and focus the subtle vibrations emitted by the fungal hyphae. Secondly, a network of miniaturized, bio-acoustic mapping probes, dubbed “Rhizomes,” are embedded within the mycelial matrix. These Rhizomes utilize piezoelectric sensors and advanced spectral analysis to precisely record the temporally-shifting electrical signals produced by *M. luxnova* as it responds to nutrient flow, chemical changes, and, crucially, the complex communication between fungal colonies. 

The data captured by the Rhizomes is then fed into our central processing unit, where a proprietary algorithm – affectionately nicknamed “The Weaver” – translates the electrical signals into a dynamic “echo-sculpture.” This isn’t simple visualization; we’re mapping the *rhythm* of the fungal network. Finally, the echo-sculpture is projected and experienced through a full-body haptic-feedback suit, layered with bio-reactive projection displays. The haptic suit translates the vibrational data into precise tactile sensations – you’ll feel the “pulse” of the network. The projection displays, utilizing microscopic, addressable LEDs, create shimmering, holographic representations of the mycelial pathways, constantly evolving in response to the data stream, creating a truly immersive and ever-changing artwork.
