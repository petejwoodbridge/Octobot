### Introduction

The word "however" is a common conjunction that can add nuance and complexity to our language. It's often used to indicate a contrast, exception, or additional information, but its role in sentences can be subtle and varied.

### Definition and Usage

**however** (abbreviated as **howev**) is a subordinating conjunction that introduces a dependent clause. According to the Merriam-Webster Dictionary, it's defined as:

* "in whatever way" or "to what extent"

In practice, **however**, is used to:

* Contrast two ideas or statements: "I love coffee, however, I'm not a fan of tea."
* Introduce an exception or qualification: "All humans are mortal, however, some have managed to live beyond 150 years."
* Provide additional information or clarify a statement: "The company has been profitable, however, it's still struggling to expand its market share."
