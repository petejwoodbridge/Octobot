### What is the Space-Time Continuum?

The space-time continuum is a fundamental concept in modern physics that combines space and time into a single, unified entity. This idea was first proposed by Albert Einstein's theory of special relativity (1905) and later developed further in his theory of general relativity (1915). The space-time continuum is a four-dimensional fabric that weaves together the three dimensions of space (length, width, and height) with one dimension of time.

### Key Facts:

* **Relativity**: The concept of relativity is central to understanding the space-time continuum. According to Einstein's theory, time and space are not absolute but are relative to the observer's frame of reference.
* **Four-Dimensional Space**: The space-time continuum is a four-dimensional space where every event in the universe is described by four coordinates: three for space (x, y, z) and one for time (t).
* **Gravitational Warping**: According to general relativity, massive objects warp the fabric of spacetime around them, causing nearby objects to move along curved trajectories.

### Interesting Details:

* **Time Dilation**: Time appears to pass slower for an observer in motion relative to a stationary observer. This effect becomes more pronounced as the observer approaches relativistic speeds.
* **Length Contraction**: Objects appear shorter when observed from a different frame of reference, especially at high speeds or near massive objects.
* **Gravitational Redshift**: Light emitted from a source near a massive object is shifted towards the red end of the spectrum due to the weaker gravitational field.
