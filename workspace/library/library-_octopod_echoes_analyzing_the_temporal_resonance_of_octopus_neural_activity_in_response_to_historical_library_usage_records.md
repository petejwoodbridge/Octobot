### Background & Context – The Octopod Neural Framework

*   **Octopus Neuroanatomy:** Octopuses possess a highly decentralized nervous system. Roughly 90% of their neurons are located in their arms, each capable of relatively independent processing. This contrasts sharply with the centralized nervous systems of mammals. This distributed architecture is believed to be key to their complex behaviors – camouflage, manipulation, and sophisticated problem-solving.
*   **Sensory Perception:** Octopuses primarily rely on chemoreception (taste and smell) and mechanoreception (touch) for navigating and interacting with their environment. Their lateral line system, sensitive to water movement, is also a crucial sensory modality. Crucially, studies indicate a sensitivity to electromagnetic fields - a potentially relevant factor in our research.
*   **Neural Plasticity:** Octopuses exhibit significant neural plasticity, demonstrating the ability to reorganize their nervous systems in response to experience. This plasticity suggests a capacity for learning and potentially, for retaining “memories” of past events.
