1.  **Mapping & Reconstruction:** Historians and digital artists collaborate to create incredibly detailed 3D models of specific historical locations and events. These models aren’t just visual; they’re infused with detailed sensory data – historical weather patterns, prevalent smells (replicated via micro-diffusion technology), typical sounds (synthesized based on historical records and acoustic modeling), and even simulated ground textures.
2.  **Vibration Resonance:** The Chronos Engine analyzes the data and transmits precisely timed vibrational patterns to the pod’s micro-actuators. 3.  **Sensory Layering:** Simultaneously, the sensory emitters release controlled bursts of historically accurate scents (created from natural extracts and synthesized compounds) and subtly adjust the ambient temperature within the pod to match the conditions of the recreated environment. 
4.  **User Interface:** A neural interface headband, utilizing advanced EEG monitoring, allows the user to subtly influence the simulation – focusing on a specific individual within the scene, shifting the temporal perspective, or triggering interactive elements based on their observed behavior.
