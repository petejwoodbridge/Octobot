## How It Works
The core of the Litho-Emotive Echo Chamber is a specially formulated bio-reactive concrete infused with microscopic, light-sensitive crystals of layered silicates. The user wears a discreet biometric suit equipped with sensors measuring heart rate variability, skin conductance, muscle tension, and even subtle EEG readings to detect emotional states – primarily focusing on identifying levels of joy, sadness, anger, and fear. This data is fed into a micro-controller which, in turn, adjusts the illumination of precisely targeted LED arrays embedded within the concrete. 

These LEDs trigger a controlled chemical reaction within the silicates, causing localized expansion and contraction of the crystal structure. This physical movement subtly reshapes the concrete, creating organic, flowing forms. The speed and intensity of these changes are directly correlated to the emotional data.  A surge of joy might result in rapid upward spirals; intense sadness could manifest as drooping, shadowed areas. The “Echo Chamber” aspect comes from the concrete retaining a slight “memory” of its sculpted state – essentially, the chemical reactions are slowed enough to maintain the form for a period, creating a visible echo of the emotion.  The entire process is housed within a polished titanium shell for durability and aesthetic appeal.  Finally, high-resolution cameras capture the changing form, creating a time-lapse video documenting the emotional journey.
