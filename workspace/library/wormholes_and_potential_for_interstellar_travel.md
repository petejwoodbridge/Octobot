## What are Wormholes?
A wormhole is a hypothetical shortcut through spacetime, connecting two distant points in space. Imagine a tunnel or tube that allows matter and energy to travel from one point to another, potentially bypassing significant distances. This concept arises from Einstein's theory of general relativity, which describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime.

## Theoretical Background
Wormholes are thought to be stabilized by a type of exotic matter with negative energy density. This "negative mass" would have opposite properties to regular matter, allowing it to exert negative pressure and maintain the wormhole's structure. However, the existence of such exotic matter is still purely theoretical.

### Types of Wormholes
There are two main types of wormholes:

* **Einstein-Rosen wormholes**: These are hypothetical connections between two points in spacetime, potentially traversable by objects with sufficient negative energy density.
* **Quantum wormholes**: These are thought to be temporary, quantum-mechanical connections between two points, allowing for non-local information transfer.
