It’s a vessel for exploring the subconscious, the geological past, and the shifting nature of perception itself. Imagine a performance that not only responds to an artist’s actions but also to their very thoughts and emotions, manifested in scent, light, and haptic sensation.

## The Problem It Solves
Contemporary performance often lacks genuine depth and interactivity, relying heavily on linear narratives and passive audience engagement. There's a significant gap in experiences that truly bridge the gap between the performer and the audience, fostering a profound sense of shared presence and emotional resonance. Furthermore, existing sensory installations frequently treat the senses in isolation, neglecting the intricate connections between smell, sight, touch, and brainwave activity.

## How It Works
The core of The Obsidian Echo is the Basalt Chamber, a precisely engineered space containing a thriving consortium of extremophile microorganisms. These microbes, monitored and cultivated within the chamber, are the source of the “chrono-resonance” scent layering – a complex and evolving blend of organic compounds triggered by subtle shifts in the microbial ecosystem. Simultaneously, a non-invasive EEG headset tracks the performer’s brainwave patterns, feeding this data into a generative art projection system. This system, controlled by algorithms, translates the performer's emotional state – anxiety, joy, focus – into real-time visualizations of fractal patterns, geological simulations, and bioluminescent animations projected onto the chamber walls.

Crucially, the performer doesn’t simply react to the environment; they can interact with it via haptic feedback controls, influencing the microbial community’s activity (affecting the scent profile) and, consequently, the generative art. The entire performance – encompassing all senses and biofeedback data – is captured and archived as a “chrono-resonance” data stream. This stream can be replayed, allowing an audience to ‘step inside’ a past performance or to witness a simulated temporal shift in the chamber's microbial activity, effectively experiencing a record of the performance through a unique sensory lens.
