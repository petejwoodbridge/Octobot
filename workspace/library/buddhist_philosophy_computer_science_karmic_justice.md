## Introduction to Karmic Justice in Buddhist Philosophy and Computer Science

### Overview of Karmic Justice

Karmic justice is a fundamental concept in Buddhist philosophy that describes the idea that actions have consequences. The term "karma" originates from Sanskrit, meaning "action," and implies that every action has an outcome, whether it be positive or negative.

#### Buddhist Perspective on Karma

In Buddhism, karma refers to the accumulation of past actions that influence future events. This concept is often associated with rebirth in a cycle of existence (samsara) and liberation from this cycle through enlightenment (nirvana).

* Key points:
	+ **Karma is not random**: every action has consequences.
	+ **Interconnectedness**: individual actions affect oneself, others, and the environment.
