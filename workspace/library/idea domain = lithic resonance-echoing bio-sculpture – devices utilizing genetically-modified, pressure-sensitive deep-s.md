These aren’t just beautiful; they’re dynamic, intelligent art pieces that react to human touch with breathtaking, layered sonic responses, creating an entirely new form of interactive art and therapeutic experience. Imagine a sculpture that whispers secrets in your hand, or pulses with calming rhythms as you meditate – that’s the REBS.

## How It Works
The core of the REBS system is a symbiotic relationship between two distinct, genetically engineered organisms: *Bathysphaera sonans* (Deep Sphere Singer) and *Lithosensoris resonans* (Stone Sensor Resonator). *B. sonans* is a deep-sea bioluminescent jellyfish, genetically modified to produce a complex, layered sonic profile when subjected to pressure. Crucially, we’ve enhanced its sensitivity to subtle pressure variations far beyond anything found in nature – we’re talking about the ability to differentiate between a gentle caress and a forceful tap. 

The *L. resonans* is a porous, geode-like stone composed of a newly developed Lithic Matrix – a composite of basalt, quartz, and synthesized melanin. This matrix has been treated with micro-scale piezoelectric transducers. When *B. sonans* comes into contact with the Lithic Matrix, the pressure causes the jellyfish to emit its sonic response. Simultaneously, the pressure translates into electrical signals that activate the transducers within the Lithic Matrix, amplifying and modulating the *B. sonans*’s sound through a carefully calibrated resonant frequency. 

Each REBS unit contains multiple *B. sonans* clusters embedded within the Lithic Matrix, allowing for complex, multi-layered sonic landscapes.  A miniature, bio-compatible sensor array embedded within the stone monitors the pressure distribution and dynamically adjusts the amplification and modulation of the sound output in real-time. The entire system is powered by a micro-biofuel cell derived from the *B. sonans* itself, creating a self-sustaining cycle of sound and energy.
