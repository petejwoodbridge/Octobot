“EchoStone: A handheld device that analyzes the vibrational signature of ancient stone tools to generate a layered olfactory reconstruction of the historical context – a 'scent-map' of the past. It uses a combination of high-resolution spectroscopy, piezoelectric sensors, and a miniature, highly controlled bio-reactor to synthesize volatile organic compounds (VOCs) based on the tool's vibrational data. The user wears a haptic suit that subtly mimics the texture and weight of the original tool, providing a tactile connection to the past. The device displays a 3D holographic projection of the tool, overlaid with a dynamic scent cloud representing the historical context.”
