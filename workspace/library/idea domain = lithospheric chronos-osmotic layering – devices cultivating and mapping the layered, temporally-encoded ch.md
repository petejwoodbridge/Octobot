## How It Works

These sensors are strategically placed across the suit’s surface – concentrated on the torso, back, and limbs – to capture a broad range of seismic and acoustic signals. This processor utilizes advanced algorithms, developed through machine learning analysis of geological survey data and historical seismic recordings, to translate these raw signals into complex soundscapes. 

Crucially, the soundscapes aren't simply recordings of seismic activity. They are *reconstructed* using a custom-built synthesizer that mimics the natural harmonic frequencies of rock formations, water movement, and atmospheric pressure. The intensity and timbre of the sound are dynamically adjusted based on the magnitude and frequency of the detected vibrations. The suit also incorporates subtle haptic feedback – gentle vibrations – to further enhance the user's sense of immersion. Users can adjust the intensity of the experience and select different ‘geological profiles’ – pre-programmed responses to specific types of terrain (e.g., “Alpine Fault Line,” “Amazon Rainforest Basin”).
