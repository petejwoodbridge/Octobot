## How It Works
GeoEcho utilizes a patented “Resonance Matrix” – a miniature array of ultra-sensitive, piezoelectric transducers housed within a durable, weatherproof casing. The core technology involves three key stages. First, the device’s ‘Scanner Head’ – resembling a stylized geologist’s hammer – is brought into contact with the eroding rock surface. As the rock vibrates due to wind, temperature fluctuations, or subtle seismic activity, the piezoelectric transducers within the Resonance Matrix instantly convert these vibrations into electrical signals. 

Secondly, this raw data is fed into the onboard “Geo-Processor,” a custom-designed microcomputer running proprietary algorithms based on years of research into rock fracturing, weathering patterns, and vibrational physics. The Geo-Processor analyzes the vibrational ‘fingerprint’ – identifying dominant frequencies, harmonic complexities, and temporal shifts – and translates them into a 3D textural map. This map isn't just a visual representation; it’s a vibrational blueprint!

Finally, the Geo-Processor directs a ‘Micro-Sculpting Unit’ – a rapidly-moving array of micro-jets propelled by compressed air – to deposit a precisely-controlled granular material (ranging from sandstone dust to ceramic aggregates) onto a receiving surface, recreating the translated texture in real-time. The speed and precision of the Micro-Sculpting Unit, controlled by the Geo-Processor, allow for the creation of textures down to a microscopic level. Multiple GeoEcho devices can be networked to capture a larger area and create larger-scale textures.
