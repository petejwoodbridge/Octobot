## Overview

## The Problem It Solves
Archaeologists, geologists, and even historical preservationists currently rely on laborious and subjective methods to understand a site's ancient environment. Traditional carbon dating and soil analysis provide context, but lack the sensory richness to truly *feel* the conditions of the past. Furthermore, identifying subtle changes in soil composition linked to past events (e.g., ancient wildfires, erosion patterns, or even lost settlements) is incredibly difficult.

## How It Works
Chronoscent utilizes a miniaturized, multi-stage system based on bio-reactive polymer technology. These polymers react with the VOCs present in the sample, creating distinct color changes and electrical signals. The device's integrated spectrometer then analyzes these changes, identifying the dominant VOCs – methane, benzene, toluene, terpenes, etc. – and their relative concentrations.

This isn’t simply a 'forest floor' scent; it’s the *temporal* scent of the forest floor as it existed 5,000 years ago, or 100 years ago, or at the moment of a volcanic eruption. The release rate of the scent is controlled by a built-in chronometer, creating a timed "scent layer" that slowly dissipates, simulating the evolving geological conditions. The device also logs the VOC data for analysis and archiving.
