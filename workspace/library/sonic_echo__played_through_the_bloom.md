## The Problem It Solves

The frustrating reality of “sonic echo” – the fleeting, distorted remnants of a sound bouncing off surfaces – is rarely a desirable effect. It’s often a nuisance in recording studios, a distracting annoyance in architectural design, or simply an irritating reverb in an empty room. Current methods of dampening or manipulating echo are often bulky, expensive, or imprecise. There’s no elegant, immediate way to control or *capture* these sonic ghosts.

## How It Works

Here’s the process:  You hold the EchoBloom near a surface experiencing sonic echo. The app allows the user to subtly shift the ‘seed’’s frequency or amplitude, effectively sculpting and preserving the echo’s unique characteristics.
