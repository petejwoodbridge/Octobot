## How It Works
The Chronoshell utilizes a combination of advanced sensor technology and bio-luminescent algae. The core is a spherical pod constructed from reinforced, impact-resistant bio-plastic. Embedded within the pod are:

1.  **Geological Mapping Array:** A network of micro-sensors constantly monitors ground vibration, temperature gradients, subtle shifts in magnetic field, and atmospheric pressure – all indicators of geological activity.
2.  **Bio-Luminescent Data Capture:** Genetically engineered bioluminescent algae, housed in a surrounding gel matrix, react directly to the geological data. For example, a spike in seismic activity triggers an increase in the algae’s luminescence, mapped and recorded in 3D. Different geological elements (mineral deposits, water tables) would each influence a unique color and intensity of light.
3.  **Localized Microclimate Generator:** A thermoelectric device subtly manipulates the pod's internal temperature, mimicking the temperature conditions at the time of recording.
4.  **Acoustic Imprint System:** Highly sensitive microphones capture ambient sounds – wind, water, animal calls – and, using sophisticated AI, reconstructs a 3D audio landscape based on the geological data, layering the sounds with the visual data.

The data is stored within the pod and can be replayed through a VR headset, projecting the reconstructed environment and triggering the sensory outputs simultaneously.
