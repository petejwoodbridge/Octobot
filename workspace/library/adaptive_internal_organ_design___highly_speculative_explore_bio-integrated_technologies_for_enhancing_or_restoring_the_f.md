5. **Self-Repair:** When damage occurs within the Heartweave, the mycelium rapidly proliferates, effectively “patching” the affected area.

Liver failure is a leading cause of death worldwide, and current treatments—transplant waiting lists, dialysis, medication—are often limited by supply, access, and side effects. Symbiotic Echo offers a dynamically adaptive solution, bypassing the limitations of traditional organ repair by directly mimicking nature’s incredibly efficient regeneration process.

2.  **Implantable Housing:** This housing, 3D-printed from a biocompatible ceramic infused with gold nanoparticles for conductivity, houses the coral micro-network. It's precisely fitted to the patient’s liver via minimally invasive surgery and secured with a bio-adhesive.

Using advanced EEG analysis, it identifies patterns of tissue degradation and adjusts the pulse frequency and intensity of the coral micro-network’s bioluminescence accordingly. The neuro-stimulator learns the patient’s liver’s unique ‘rhythm’ for optimal stimulation.
