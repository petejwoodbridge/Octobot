These structures manipulate light, contributing to iridescent effects and enhancing camouflage. They aren't just about color; they control *how* light is reflected.
* **Neural Control & Pattern Generation:**  Crucially, cephalopod camouflage isn't random. Research suggests they “see” their surroundings not just with eyes, but through vibrations and pressure changes in the water.
* **Evolutionary Significance:** Cephalopod camouflage evolved primarily for predator avoidance and, in some cases, ambush predation. The patterns aren’t purely decorative; they're deeply rooted in survival.
