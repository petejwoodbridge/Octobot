## What is General Relativity?
------------------------------

General relativity (GR) is a theory of gravitation developed by Albert Einstein in 1915. It postulates that gravity is not a force acting between objects, but rather the curvature of spacetime caused by massive objects. According to GR, any object with mass or energy warps the fabric of spacetime around it, resulting in the phenomenon we experience as gravity.

### Key Features of General Relativity
-----------------------------------------

* **Curvature of Spacetime**: Massive objects warp spacetime, causing it to curve and bend.
* **Equivalence Principle**: The effects of gravity are equivalent to the effects of acceleration.
* **Geodesics**: Paths that follow the shortest distance through curved spacetime.

### Implications of General Relativity
------------------------------------------

* **Black Holes**: Extremely dense objects with such strong gravity that not even light can escape.
* **Gravitational Waves**: Ripples in spacetime caused by massive objects accelerating or decelerating.
* **Cosmology**: GR helps explain the evolution and expansion of the universe, including the Big Bang theory.

## Interesting Details
------------------------

* **GR's Origins**: Einstein developed GR as a solution to two problems: (1) reconciling Newtonian gravity with electromagnetism, and (2) explaining the perihelion precession of Mercury's orbit.
* **Mathematical Challenges**: Developing GR required Einstein to reformulate classical mechanics using tensor analysis, leading to the famous equation E=mc².
* **Experimental Confirmations**: Numerous experiments have confirmed predictions made by GR, including gravitational waves and the bending of light around massive objects.
