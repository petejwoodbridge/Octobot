## The Problem It Solves
Current VR and AR technologies primarily rely on visual immersion, leaving other senses largely unaddressed. This results in a disconnect between the virtual world and reality, leading to motion sickness, cognitive strain, and a limited sense of presence. Furthermore, many therapeutic applications – like exposure therapy or pain management – struggle to realistically replicate the complex sensory input needed for effective treatment.

## How It Works

These algae are engineered to react to specific digital signals, allowing them to emit light at pre-programmed frequencies and intensities. Second, a miniature, wearable processor (integrated into a comfortable wristband) acts as the ‘brain’, receiving data from a paired neural interface (a comfortable, discreet headband that monitors brainwave activity) and the user's desired environmental settings. The neural interface analyzes the user's emotional state and adjusts the algae’s light patterns to create an appropriate visual experience. Simultaneously, the processor sends signals to the micro-fluidic channels, releasing scents (e.g., pine needles for a forest scene, saltwater for a beach) and adjusting the garment’s temperature – a slight chill for a snowy mountain, a gentle warmth for a tropical sunset – enhancing the illusion. Advanced algorithms ensure seamless transitions between environments and maintain a stable, believable sensory profile.
