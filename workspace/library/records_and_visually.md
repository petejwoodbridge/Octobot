## Overview

By analyzing the sonic landscape of a recording—voice, music, ambient noise—the sculpture generates a breathtaking, living artwork that mirrors the emotional and structural qualities of the sound itself, offering a profoundly visual-based embodiment of auditory experience.

## The Problem It Solves
Traditional music visualization often feels static and simplistic, primarily relying on spectrograms or oscilloscope-like representations. It fails to truly capture the nuances, emotional weight, and structural complexity of a recording. Furthermore, many people struggle to *feel* a connection to music beyond intellectual understanding – the sensory experience is frequently missing. EchoBloom aims to bridge this gap by providing a tangible, visceral reaction to sound.
