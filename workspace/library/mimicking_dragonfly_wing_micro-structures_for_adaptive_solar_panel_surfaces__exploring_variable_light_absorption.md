## How It Works

The IrisPan utilizes micro-fabricated, iridescent scales – inspired by the precise nano-structure of dragonfly wings – to achieve variable light absorption. Each scale is composed of a layer of dichroic film (similar to those used in high-end stage lighting) embedded within a flexible, bio-compatible polymer. These scales are not individually movable; instead, they are arranged in a tessellated grid across the solar panel surface. 

Here’s the clever part:  Each scale has a built-in microfluidic channel filled with a thermochromic liquid. This liquid changes colour based on temperature.  A tiny, integrated thermoelectric generator (TEG) – powered by the solar panel itself – subtly heats and cools these channels. By precisely controlling the temperature of the fluid within each scale, we create a dynamic iridescent effect.

When sunlight is bright and direct, the thermochromic fluid remains cool, creating a shimmering, almost holographic display of colour. This alters the dichroic film’s reflection, maximizing absorption across the entire solar spectrum. As sunlight becomes diffused or angled, the fluid warms slightly, shifting the colour palette and prioritizing absorption of lower-angle, less intense light. The overall effect is a constantly shifting, mesmerizing surface – visually beautiful and incredibly effective at capturing energy. Furthermore, the entire structure is self-cleaning due to the microfluidic action.
