## Overview

Let’s face it: spilling beans is a universally relatable frustration. Whether it’s a rogue pinto during a chili-making emergency, a cascade of chickpeas while prepping hummus, or a scattering of lentils during a particularly enthusiastic stir-fry, the cleanup is always messy, time-consuming, and often results in a significant portion of the beans being lost. Current solutions – awkwardly reaching, using chopsticks, or employing sticky substances – are inefficient and frequently fail, particularly when dealing with uneven surfaces or height.

Once the bean’s location and trajectory are calculated, the BRR autonomously navigates to the bean, gently captures it using the gripper, and retracts the arm back into the drone’s chassis. A small internal conveyor system then delivers the bean to a miniature, removable collection chamber. The drone is powered by a miniature, rechargeable battery and controlled via a smartphone app for manual override and mapping review.
