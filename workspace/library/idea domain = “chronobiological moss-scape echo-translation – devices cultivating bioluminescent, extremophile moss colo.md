## How It Works

The Echo Bloom operates on a multi-layered system centered around *Cryosynthetica lux*, a newly identified strain of extremophile moss. This moss, sourced from the perpetually dark, geothermal vents of Iceland, possesses an extraordinary ability to ‘remember’ its environment – essentially, it stores information within its cellular structure. 

The device itself is a spherical, geodesic dome constructed from recycled aerogel and reinforced with a bio-polymer grown by engineered mycelium. Inside, a series of concentric rings house the core technology:

1.  **Chronobiological Sensors:** These rings are lined with highly sensitive piezoelectric sensors that detect subtle changes in atmospheric pressure, temperature, humidity, and, crucially, sonic vibrations. A sophisticated algorithm, developed by analyzing seismic data and historical weather patterns, translates these vibrations into specific rhythmic pulses.
2.  **Bio-Resonance Chamber:** At the heart of the dome is a controlled environment chamber maintaining precisely 45°C and 95% humidity – optimal for *Cryosynthetica lux*. This chamber contains a nutrient solution infused with trace minerals and, most importantly, targeted “temporal seeds” – microscopic capsules containing modified bacterial cultures. These bacteria, triggered by the sonic pulses, stimulate the moss’s bio-recording process.
3. **Bioluminescent Cascade:** The moss itself doesn't just glow; it *translates*. The rhythmic pulses trigger a cascading bioluminescent reaction within the moss colonies, producing a dazzling display of light – a shifting spectrum of blues, greens, and purples – directly correlated to the sensed environmental data. The intensity and color of the light are controlled by micro-fluidic channels that modulate the flow of a bioluminescent compound synthesized within the moss itself. Furthermore, an integrated neural network analyzes the overall light display and subtly adjusts the sonic pulses, creating a feedback loop that amplifies the moss’s responsiveness.
