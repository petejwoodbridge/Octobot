Simultaneously, the Temporal Resonance Mapping system – a quantum-entangled fractal generator – analyzes the user’s personal chronobiological data (gathered from wearable biosensors over a period of weeks) to identify ‘temporal hotspots’ – moments of heightened emotional or sensory activity. 

Once identified, the CEES generates a precisely modulated acoustic field, utilizing a series of phased, binaural beats and subtle infrasound frequencies. These aren't just any sounds; they’re “echoes” – reconstructions of the sensory data associated with the target memory, layered with holographic projections of subtly shifting light patterns calibrated to induce a state of focused recall. Crucially, the device doesn't simply *play back* the memory. The user actively *sculpts* the echo through a haptic interface – a series of pressure-sensitive pads – allowing them to adjust the intensity, clarity, and even emotional valence of the experience. For example, a user recalling a joyful picnic could ‘dial up’ the warmth of the sun or ‘soften’ the feeling of anxiety associated with a particular conversation.
