## Overview
ChronoScent is a handheld device that allows users to ‘listen’ to the geological past through its scent. By inducing controlled, localized seismic events within a rock sample, it unlocks and translates the volatile organic compounds released – recreating the atmospheric scent profile of that specific location as it existed millions, even billions, of years ago.

## The Problem It Solves
Paleontology and geology face a fundamental limitation: we can only infer the past environments based on fossil records and limited geochemical analysis. Current methods struggle to capture the complete sensory experience of a past landscape, preventing a truly immersive understanding of ancient ecosystems and planetary evolution. Furthermore, current methods of environmental reconstruction are limited to *current* conditions, and cannot effectively extrapolate the atmospheric and olfactory profile of long-gone periods.

## How It Works
The ChronoScent utilizes a miniaturized, pulsed piezoelectric transducer array housed within a durable, impact-resistant casing. This array is targeted at a specific rock sample via a directional sonic beam. The key innovation lies in the calibration of the sonic pulses – initially, the pulses are low-amplitude and carefully sequenced to induce *microseismic events* within the rock strata, specifically targeting areas rich in zeolite minerals and fractured limestone. These events don’t cause significant damage; they’re precisely controlled, measurable vibrations.

As the rock fractures, it releases trapped VOCs – methane, hydrogen sulfide, terpenes, and other compounds – which were previously locked within the mineral matrix. Simultaneously, a highly sensitive micro-chromatography and mass spectrometry (MS) system, integrated within the device, rapidly analyzes these released compounds in real-time. Critically, this data isn't just recorded; it’s translated into a complex sonic signature unique to the chemical composition and volume of the released VOCs.

This sonic signature is then fed into a miniature, directional scent diffusion system – a micro-nebulizer – which releases the VOCs in a carefully modulated manner, recreating the original scent profile. The device’s integrated gyroscope and accelerometer track the user’s movement, dynamically adjusting the scent output to simulate the perceived spatial location of the original geological formation.  The entire process takes approximately 15 seconds per "scan."
