These allow for rapid, dynamic changes in color and pattern – shifting from mottled camouflage to bold displays in milliseconds.  Crucially, the *precise* shade and hue achieved aren't random; they’re meticulously calculated.
*   **Contextual Color Coding:** Research suggests cephalopods don't simply ‘copy’ a color; they encode it based on the surrounding environment. This creates highly complex, spatially-redundant color data. Think of it like a super-detailed, dynamically-updated texture map.

This is remarkably efficient for images with flowing water or dynamic movement.
*   **“Chromatic Signature” Generation:** The most advanced models utilize a “chromatic signature” – a mathematically-defined representation of an image’s color frequency distribution.
