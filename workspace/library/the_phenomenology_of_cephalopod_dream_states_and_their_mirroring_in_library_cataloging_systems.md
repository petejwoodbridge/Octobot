## The Phenomenology of Cephalopod Dream States

*   **"Phantom" Prey:** Octopuses have been observed reacting to stimuli – a simulated crab, for example – even when the stimulus is removed. This suggests the brain is rehearsing hunting strategies and maintaining an active, internally generated representation of a threat.
*   **Spatial Mapping:** Cephalopods create remarkably detailed 3D maps of their environment within their brains. The resting arm movements likely contribute to the maintenance and updating of these spatial maps. 
*   **REM-like Sleep (Speculation):** While not definitively proven, there's growing speculation that cephalopods experience something analogous to REM sleep, characterized by rapid eye movements and the heightened neural activity described above.
