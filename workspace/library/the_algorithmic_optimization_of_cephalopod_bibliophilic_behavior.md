*   **Widespread Replication:** Subsequent observations across various octopus and squid species (including common octopus *Octopus vulgaris*) demonstrated a recurring pattern: a drawn-out period of investigation followed by a return to, and prolonged interaction with, printed materials.
*   **Behavioral Patterns:** This isn't just random attraction. Octopuses engage in:

    *   **Smell Detection:**  Books contain a complex mixture of inks, paper fibers, and, importantly, residual human scent – a major attractant. Research suggests they can differentiate between paper types based on volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
    *   **Object Permanence Investigation:** They seem to engage in a primitive “object permanence” experiment, repeatedly searching for the book after it’s been moved, suggesting an understanding of its continued existence.
