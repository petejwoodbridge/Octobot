### Handwritten Marginalia: A Complex Pattern

Handwritten marginalia, particularly from the 15th-19th centuries, represents a rich data set of organic patterns. The factors influencing decay include:

*   **Ink Composition:** Iron gall ink (common in early printed books) is notoriously unstable and susceptible to oxidation, leading to fading and discoloration.
*   **Paper Type:** Different paper fibers have varying levels of cellulose degradation rates.
*   **Writing Style:** The pressure applied, the speed of writing, and even the slant of the script influence ink flow and paper damage.
*   **Environmental Factors:** Temperature, humidity, and light exposure accelerate degradation.
