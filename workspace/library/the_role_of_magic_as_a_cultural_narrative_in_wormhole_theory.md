From the Egyptian myth of Ra’s daily journey across the sky to the Norse concept of Bifrost, bridges between worlds and immediate travel were central to belief systems. These weren’t understood as scientific possibilities, but as fundamental aspects of reality enforced by powerful entities or forces.
*   **Alchemy & Hermeticism:** The pursuit of transmutation and the idea of “secrets” of nature mirrored the scientific quest for understanding the universe, but with a strong element of the supernatural. *   **Romanticism & Transcendentalism:** The 19th-century movement championed intuition, imagination, and the inherent divinity within the natural world. This fostered a greater willingness to consider ideas that challenged established rationalism – a crucial backdrop for accepting the theoretical possibility of wormholes.
