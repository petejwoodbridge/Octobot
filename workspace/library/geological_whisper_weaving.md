“Project: EchoStone - A subterranean data-sonification system. EchoStone consists of a network of bio-acoustic sensors deployed within abandoned mines, designed to detect and interpret the vibrational patterns and chemical secretions of subterranean fungal networks. These signals are then translated into a dynamic, multi-sensory experience – primarily an immersive augmented reality projection overlaid onto the mine’s walls. The projection isn't just a static image; it *flows* and *evolves* in real-time, responding to the fungal data. Think swirling patterns of light and color, subtly shifting textures, and echoing sonic layers. A secondary output is a physical, sculpted soundscape generated by custom-built resonant chambers - like giant, throbbing, bioluminescent organs of sound. Users wear haptic suits that translate the projected data into tactile sensations – a gentle pulse mirroring a seismic shift, a subtle vibration reflecting changes in fungal growth. We’ll even integrate a scent layer – carefully calibrated microbial cultures emitting specific aromas triggered by the data analysis – representing the mineral composition. The goal isn't just data visualization, but a visceral, emotional connection with the earth’s hidden networks. The 'music' isn’t just sound, it’s the *feeling* of geological time!”
