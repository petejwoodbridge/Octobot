Anxiety, depression, and chronic stress often manifest with a lack of grounding and difficulty accessing a state of calm. ## How It Works
The garment itself is constructed from a tightly woven, breathable fabric infused with *Psilocybe Cubensis* mycelium – ethically sourced and carefully cultivated to a specific density. This mycelial network isn’t just passively absorbing scents; it’s actively producing them through a complex biocommunication process. Embedded sensors within the fabric continuously monitor the wearer’s biofeedback data: heart rate variability (HRV), skin conductance (GSR), brainwave activity (EEG via subtle, non-invasive sensors), and even minute muscle tension. 

This data feeds into a micro-controller that dictates the ‘scent profile’ generated by the mycelium. The mycelium, stimulated by electrical pulses mirroring the detected biofeedback, releases specific volatile organic compounds (VOCs) – not in a harsh, synthetic way, but in a layered, evolving cascade. For example:

*   **High Anxiety (Elevated GSR, Increased Heart Rate):** The mycelium releases calming scents like sandalwood, lavender, and vetiver, gradually shifting towards more grounding notes like cedarwood and moss as the wearer’s HRV improves.
*   **Low Mood (Decreased HRV, Slow Brainwaves):** The device might introduce uplifting scents like citrus, peppermint, and rosemary, slowly transitioning to scents associated with focus and creativity like frankincense and myrrh.
*   **Stress (Muscle Tension Detected):** Gentle, tension-releasing scents like chamomile and ylang-ylang would be prioritized.

The entire process is controlled by a companion app that allows users to customize scent preferences and track their biometric data alongside the scentscapes, creating a powerful feedback loop.
