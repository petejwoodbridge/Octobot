## Einstein-Rosen Bridges
### Definition
Einstein-Rosen bridges, also known as Schwarzschild wormholes, are hypothetical tunnels through spacetime that connect two points in the universe. These bridges are predicted by general relativity and are thought to be stabilized by exotic matter with negative energy density.

### Characteristics
* One-way passage: Only allow for travel from one point to another, not back.
* No communication possible: Information cannot be transmitted through the wormhole.
* Stability issues: Require exotic matter with negative energy density to maintain stability.
* Potential applications: Useful for understanding black hole physics and cosmology.

### Interesting Detail
The concept of Einstein-Rosen bridges was first proposed by Albert Einstein and Nathan Rosen in 1935. They suggested that a wormhole could connect two distant points, potentially allowing for faster-than-light travel. However, their solution required exotic matter with negative energy density, which is still purely theoretical.

## Traversable Wormholes
### Definition
Traversable wormholes are hypothetical tunnels through spacetime that allow for bidirectional travel and communication between two points in the universe. These wormholes are predicted by certain modified theories of gravity, such as scalar-tensor theories or brane cosmology.

### Characteristics
* Bidirectional passage: Allow for travel and communication in both directions.
* Stability dependent on matter density: The stability of traversable wormholes depends on the mass-energy density of the surrounding spacetime.
* Potential applications: Useful for understanding high-energy particle physics, cosmology, and potential solutions to the black hole information paradox.

### Interesting Detail
Traversable wormholes were first proposed by physicist Kip Thorne in 1988. He showed that certain types of matter with negative pressure could stabilize a wormhole, allowing for bidirectional travel. This idea has since been explored in various modified theories of gravity and cosmological models.
