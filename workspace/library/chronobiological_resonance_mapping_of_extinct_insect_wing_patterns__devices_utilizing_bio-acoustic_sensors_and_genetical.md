Next, the data gleaned by the BARS is fed into the GMLS. Here, we’ve genetically engineered a specialized subspecies of Luna Moth – *Lunaris Temporalis* – incorporating bioluminescent genes derived from deep-sea anglerfish and a novel olfactory receptor array designed to decode the ancient scent profiles associated with the insect’s environment. These moths aren't just beautiful; they’re living, breathing spectrometers. They’re programmed to release a precise sequence of bioluminescent pulses, dictated by the BARS data, mimicking the vibrational and chemical patterns identified in the fossil.

Finally, the entire sensory reconstruction is experienced through the PCS – a full-body haptic-feedback suit woven with micro-actuators and lined with temperature-regulating sensors. As the GMLS releases its bioluminescent ‘echoes’ and the BARS transmits its vibrational data, the PCS translates this information into precisely calibrated tactile sensations – simulating the air pressure of the insect's flight, the temperature of the surrounding environment, even subtle shifts in wind currents. The suit isn't just providing data; it’s constructing a fully realized, embodied experience. 

We've developed a proprietary ‘Chronobiological Resonance Algorithm’ (CRA) that dynamically adjusts the intensity and complexity of the sensory output, based on the estimated age and species of the fossilized insect, creating a truly authentic temporal echo.
