### Introduction

### Types of Bioluminescence
#### Chemical Reactions

Bioluminescence occurs when a chemical reaction takes place within an organism's cells. This reaction involves a molecule called luciferin, which reacts with oxygen to produce light. #### Light-Producing Proteins

Some organisms use proteins to produce bioluminescence. These proteins, such as the green fluorescent protein (GFP), emit light when exposed to certain wavelengths of light. Examples include certain types of jellyfish and squid.
