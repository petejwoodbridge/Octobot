We’re tackling a fundamental disconnect: current technology simply doesn't *listen* to the body's innate healing potential.

## How It Works
The Sonogenetic Lichen Skin Graft operates on a fascinating principle of bio-acoustic resonance and directed tissue cultivation. These fibers, when stimulated by precisely calibrated ultrasonic waves, vibrate at specific frequencies, creating a dynamic, self-assembling environment. Within this cradle, we seed a colony of *Rhizocarpon giganteum*, a particularly resilient lichen, genetically modified to express enhanced collagen production, rapid cell proliferation, and a heightened sensitivity to bio-acoustic signals. 

Crucially, the lichen isn’t merely growing; it's *responding*. A miniature biosensor, embedded within the cradle, continuously analyzes the patient's local bio-acoustic field – their heartbeat, breathing, and even subtle neural activity. This data informs the sonic resonator, which then adjusts its frequency and amplitude, effectively “speaking” to the lichen, directing its growth and shaping its cellular structure to perfectly match the damaged tissue. The ultrasonic waves stimulate the lichen to produce a complex matrix of collagen, elastin, and melanin, mimicking the natural structure and pigmentation of healthy skin. Furthermore, the lichen’s inherent symbiotic relationship with fungi within its colony actively combats bacterial infections, providing a first-line defense against pathogens. Once the graft has fully matured – typically within 72 hours – it’s seamlessly integrated into the patient’s existing tissue, continuing to self-repair and adapt, creating a truly self-healing layer.
