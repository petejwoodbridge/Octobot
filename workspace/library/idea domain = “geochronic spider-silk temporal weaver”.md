There’s a profound gap between knowing *what* happened and truly *feeling* the atmosphere, witnessing the moments, and understanding the emotional context of the past. Traditional historical sites offer limited and often biased perspectives. Furthermore, the preservation of historical locations is frequently hampered by development and neglect.

The experience isn't a perfect recreation; it’s a layered impression—visual, auditory, olfactory, and even a faint sense of emotional resonance – of the events that occurred in that location. The silk’s effect is temporary, lasting approximately 60 seconds per strand, and the spider continuously regenerates new strands.
