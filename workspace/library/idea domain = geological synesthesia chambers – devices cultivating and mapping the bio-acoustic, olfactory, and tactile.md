## How It Works
EchoStone utilizes a miniature, highly sensitive seismic sensor array encased within a bio-ceramic shell designed to mimic the properties of a natural stone. This sensor array isn’t just capturing vibrations; it’s detecting *coherent* vibrational patterns – the remnants of past geological events like earthquakes, volcanic activity, glacial movement, and even ancient meteor impacts. The device employs advanced AI, trained on a massive database of geologically-correlated seismic signatures worldwide, to decode these patterns.

The data is then translated into a multi-sensory experience. Firstly, the stone subtly vibrates at specific frequencies corresponding to the identified events, each vibration’s intensity and duration representing the energy and duration of the event. Secondly, embedded microfluidic channels within the stone release a curated blend of natural scents – pine for ancient forests, sulfur for volcanic zones, salt spray for coastal regions – reinforcing the geological context. Finally, a haptic display integrated into the stone’s surface provides a dynamic visual representation of the chronological layering, displaying the events as a rising tide or a branching tree, visually mapping the timeline. Data is wirelessly transmitted to a companion app for detailed analysis and archival.
