* **Dynamic Manipulation:** The key is *dynamic manipulation*. Octopuses don’t simply *hold* a tool; they actively transform their arm movements to suit the task at hand. They rapidly change grip strength, wrist rotation, and tentacle curvature—a level of dexterity rarely seen in other animals.
* **Sensory Feedback is Crucial:** Research using fMRI suggests octopuses use a combination of visual, tactile, and proprioceptive (sense of body position) feedback to guide their tool use. They don’t just “guess” what’s going to happen; they constantly adjust based on how the tool interacts with the environment.
* **Neural Basis – The ‘Neural Motor System’:** The octopus’s nervous system is particularly interesting. A significant portion of its neurons are dedicated to controlling its arms independently, mirroring the human brain’s motor cortex to some degree. This decentralized control allows for incredibly precise and coordinated movements.
