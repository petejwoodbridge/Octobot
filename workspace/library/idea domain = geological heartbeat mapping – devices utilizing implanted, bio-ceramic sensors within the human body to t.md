## How It Works

The GeoHeart system centers around the "TerraCell," a bio-ceramic sensor approximately the size of a grain of rice. These TerraCells are manufactured from a proprietary blend of barium titanate, strontium titanate, and trace elements harvested from volcanic rock – a critical component for optimal vibrational sensitivity! Each TerraCell is surgically implanted within the patient’s chest cavity, strategically positioned around the heart and major blood vessels, but also extending into the surrounding muscle tissue. 

Here's the magic: Each TerraCell is coated in a layer of piezoelectric material, which converts mechanical stress – including the Earth’s vibrations – into an electrical signal. These signals are then transmitted wirelessly via a miniaturized, inductively-charged transceiver (the "GeoCore") embedded in a biocompatible, flexible patch adhered to the patient’s skin. 

The GeoCore doesn’t just send data; it *processes* it. Using a complex algorithm developed in conjunction with geophysicists and neuroscientists, the GeoCore analyzes the incoming signal patterns. It identifies and isolates the Earth's vibrational signatures – specifically targeting low-frequency seismic waves, geomagnetic fluctuations, and subtle thermal variations – and correlates them with the patient's physiological data. The system creates a "Geological Heartbeat Map," a real-time visualization of the patient's body's response to the planet's rhythm, displayed on a secure, cloud-based platform for physicians. The platform will even offer predictive analytics, alerting doctors to potential anomalies *before* they manifest as traditional symptoms.
