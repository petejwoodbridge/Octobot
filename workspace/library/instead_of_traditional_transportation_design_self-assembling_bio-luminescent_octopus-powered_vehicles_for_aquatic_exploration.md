## How It Works

The Chronoshell is a bio-luminescent, self-assembling vehicle constructed primarily from a reinforced, bio-polymer grown in conjunction with genetically modified, deep-sea octopuses. These octopuses, dubbed “Luminaries,” are engineered to secrete a remarkably strong, flexible polymer – essentially, their own living scaffolding – and to exhibit controlled bioluminescence.

They then autonomously assemble, forming a spherical shell around the Core, effectively creating a mobile, bioluminescent orb. 

The Luminaries' movement isn’t driven by traditional propulsion. Instead, they manipulate water currents through rhythmic, pulsing contractions of their bodies, creating localized vortexes that propel the Chronoshell forward. The bioluminescence isn't just for show; it's integrated into the polymer, reacting to changes in water pressure and salinity to create a dynamic, three-dimensional map of the surrounding environment, relayed back to the Core. The vehicle operates entirely wirelessly, communicating through modulated bioluminescent flashes.
