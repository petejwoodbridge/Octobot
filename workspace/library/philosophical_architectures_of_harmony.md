* Heraclitean dialectics: The Greek philosopher Heraclitus famously declared "the way up and the way down are one" – highlighting the unity of opposites.
* Buddhist nondualism: Mahayana Buddhism teaches that all phenomena are interconnected and interdependent, transcending binary distinctions.

### Complementarity

Another crucial aspect is complementarity, emphasizing the interdependence of seemingly disparate elements. This concept can be seen in:

Holistic approaches focus on the interconnectedness of systems, recognizing that individual components are not isolated entities but part of a larger web. Notable examples include:

* General Systems Theory (Bertalanffy): This framework views complex systems as integrated wholes, rather than collections of separate parts.
* Gaia Hypothesis (Lovelock): The idea that the Earth's ecosystem behaves like a living, self-regulating system underscores the importance of holistic thinking.
