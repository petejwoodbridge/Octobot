Geologists painstakingly analyze rock strata, yet they can’t *smell* the heat of a long-extinct volcano or the freshness of ancient ice. The problem is a profound disconnect – we understand the *what* and *when* of geological processes, but not the *how it felt*. This leaves a critical gap in our ability to truly appreciate the immense timescales and dynamic forces that have shaped our planet.
