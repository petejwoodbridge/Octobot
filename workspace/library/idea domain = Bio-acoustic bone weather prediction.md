## How It Works
Sonaris employs a miniaturized, biocompatible sensor suite – we call them “Osteo-Nodes” – designed to be comfortably worn as a discreet wristband. Each Osteo-Node contains a layered system: 

1.  **Piezoelectric Micro-Array:** At the core is a dense array of custom-engineered piezoelectric crystals, each thinner than a human hair. These crystals convert mechanical stress (vibrations) into electrical signals.
2.  **Bio-Acoustic Resonance Filter:** The electrical signals are then fed into a sophisticated resonance filter, tuned to the specific frequencies of bone vibration. This filter isolates the signals originating from the nervous system’s response to changes in atmospheric pressure – a phenomenon we've termed “Atmospheric Resonance.”
3.  **Neural Network Decoder:** The filtered signals are processed by a miniature, low-power neural network. This network has been trained on millions of data points – correlating Osteo-Node readings with verified weather patterns over decades. Crucially, the network doesn’t *predict* weather; it *interprets* the bone’s response. 
4.  **Haptic Feedback System:** Finally, the decoded information is translated into subtle, customizable haptic feedback – a series of gentle pulses and vibrations on the wristband, representing the likelihood and intensity of weather events. A rapid, escalating pattern indicates an approaching storm, while a calm, rhythmic pulse signifies stable conditions. We’ve even incorporated a “Bone-Sync” feature, allowing users to calibrate the device to their individual bone resonance profiles for unparalleled accuracy.
