When an individual wishes to “weave” a memory, they place a small, bio-conductive “Resonance Stone” (a uniquely shaped piece of polished obsidian) onto the moss. The Stone initiates a neural scan, mapping the individual’s brainwave patterns associated with the targeted memory. This data is then fed into the micro-crystal lattice. 

The crystals, responding to the memory’s temporal resonance, begin to emit light – the *BryoPhyton lux* glows in intricate patterns, pulsating in time with the activated memory. Critically, the moss itself is engineered to respond to changes in humidity and temperature, mimicking the environmental conditions present during the original experience.  For example, if the memory is of a rainy afternoon, the moss will subtly increase humidity, and the light patterns will shift to mimic the dappled light filtering through rain clouds. 

Finally, a micro-vibration delivered through the Resonance Stone stimulates specific neural pathways associated with the memory, amplifying the sensory input and creating a powerfully immersive recall. The entire system is controlled via a sleek, biometric wristband that monitors the user's cognitive state and adjusts the resonance frequencies accordingly.
