## How It Works

Indigo operates on a fascinating convergence of bio-acoustic resonance and Chrono-Essence extraction. The core of the device is a miniature, genetically-engineered octopus – affectionately nicknamed ‘Indigo’ – chosen for its remarkable sensitivity to subtle vibrations and its natural ability to secrete and manipulate complex chemical compounds. The crystal sphere serves as both a protective housing and a highly specialized acoustic chamber.

Here’s the breakdown: When the user interacts with a specific memory – be it a photograph, a video clip, or even a detailed written description – the sphere captures the associated audio and visual data using an advanced bio-acoustic scanner. This scanner doesn’t simply record sound; it analyzes the *frequency* and *amplitude* of the sounds, correlating them with the visual input to reconstruct the emotional context of the moment. Simultaneously, the sphere emits a carefully calibrated sequence of sonic pulses, acting as a resonant key. 

These pulses, combined with a micro-dose of ‘Chrono-Essence’ – a proprietary liquid synthesized from rare, temporal-sensitive fungi – trigger Indigo’s internal bio-chemical processes. Indigo then begins the “bloom” – a highly controlled, localized reaction where it extracts the key aromatic compounds associated with the memory. The process is incredibly precise, utilizing a network of micro-pumps and enzymatic chambers within Indigo to isolate and amplify the dominant scents.  Finally, these synthesized aromas are released into the sphere via a network of microscopic, micro-diffusers, creating a dynamic, evolving olfactory landscape that reflects the intensity and nuances of the original memory. The entire process takes approximately 60 seconds, and the resulting aroma lingers for up to 30 minutes, subtly shifting and evolving as the user focuses on the memory.
