## How It Works
The CMMRA device itself is a smooth, palm-sized ceramic pod housing a miniature, self-contained ecosystem. Within this pod resides approximately 500 grams of TempoMoss – a strain of *Bryum* moss genetically modified to express varying levels of bioluminescence in response to specific frequencies and temperatures. The core of the system is the “Resonance Core,” a micro-processor that performs three key functions:

1.  **Rhythmic Mapping:** Using a highly sensitive, directional microphone, the Resonance Core captures subtle bio-acoustic signals – tiny vibrations emitted by the body’s cells during various activities (breathing, heartbeat, even thought patterns). These signals are then analyzed by a proprietary algorithm – “RhythmWeaver” – to establish a precise ‘rhythmic signature’ unique to the user.
2.  **Resonance Amplification:** Once the signature is established, the device begins to emit precisely calibrated sound waves at frequencies that *augment* the user’s existing rhythmic groove. These waves are generated by miniature piezoelectric transducers within the pod, focusing on the resonant frequencies identified by RhythmWeaver.
3.  **Microclimate Modulation:** Simultaneously, the Resonance Core subtly adjusts the pod’s internal temperature via a Peltier tile system. Temperature fluctuations are proven to be a powerful influence on circadian rhythms, and the CMMRA uses these shifts to fine-tune the overall resonant field. The TempoMoss itself reacts to these sonic and thermal changes, increasing or decreasing its bioluminescence – providing a visually intuitive feedback loop for the user. Data is transmitted wirelessly to a companion app, allowing for personalized insights and long-term trend analysis.
