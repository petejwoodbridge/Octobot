## How It Works

The EchoTune system is built around three key components: the Geo-Sensor Array, the Resonance Core, and the Sonic Projection Matrix.

Firstly, the **Geo-Sensor Array** is embedded discreetly within the building’s structure – primarily within the foundation and walls – consisting of an array of micro-accelerometers and piezoelectric sensors. These aren’t your average sensors; they’re calibrated to detect extremely subtle vibrations across a broad spectrum, from infrasound (below 20Hz) to ultrasonic (above 20kHz). They meticulously capture everything – wind gusts impacting the building, water flowing through underground channels, the seismic activity of the surrounding earth, the subtle shifts in temperature, and, crucially, the inherent resonance of the stone itself. 

Secondly, the data collected by the Geo-Sensor Array feeds into the **Resonance Core**, a miniaturized quantum processing unit. This isn't just a computer; it’s a bio-acoustic translator! Utilizing proprietary algorithms developed through decades of research into geological resonance and psychoacoustics, the Core analyzes the vibrational patterns, identifying the dominant ‘memory’ signatures.  It then generates a precise harmonic profile – essentially, a custom-designed sonic blueprint. This blueprint is determined by parameters like desired effect (focus, relaxation, creativity), time of day, and even external weather conditions. 

Finally, the **Sonic Projection Matrix** – a series of strategically placed, micro-haptic speakers woven into the building’s fabric – translates the Core’s harmonic profile into audible sound. These speakers aren’t merely playing pre-recorded loops; they’re actively modulating the building’s internal acoustics in real-time, creating a dynamic, responsive sonic landscape. Critically, the speakers use a patented phased array technology, focusing the sound waves with pinpoint accuracy to create a truly immersive and personalized auditory experience.
