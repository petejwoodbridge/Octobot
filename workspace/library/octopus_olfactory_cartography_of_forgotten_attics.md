### The Octopus Nervous System: A Distributed Computing Marvel

*   **Decentralized Intelligence:** Octopuses possess the most complex nervous system of any invertebrate. Approximately two-thirds of their neurons are distributed throughout their arms, not concentrated in a central brain. This creates a decentralized processing network – each arm essentially operates somewhat independently, receiving and interpreting sensory input.
*   **Chemoreception – Beyond Taste:** While they can taste, octopus chemoreception is dominated by olfactory receptors located in their suckers. These aren’t simply detecting general "good" or "bad" smells. They're incredibly sensitive to subtle changes in chemical composition.
*   **Ephaptic Communication:** Critically, octopuses use *ephaptic communication*. This means they physically touch surfaces with their suckers and transmit information about texture, shape, and chemical cues *through* these physical contacts. This isn’t just tactile sensing; it's a direct neurological signal.
